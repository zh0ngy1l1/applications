UC Activities and Awards

1. Bronze Medal, International Mathematical Olympiad 2024

Level of recognition: International

Type of award: Academic

Grade level when awarded: 10th grade

Award requirements: At IMO 2024, 326 medals were given, out which 145 were bronze. A bronze medal indicates placing above half the participants, and I placed 252nd with 17 as a result of solving problems 1 and 5, and getting partial solutions for problems 2 and 4.

What you did to earn award: I had started practicing specifically for the IMO on August 2023, after learning that I was selected for the Finnish team for Baltic Way 2023 in Germany based on the TST. I prepared by working on Evan Chen's EGMO and Khurmi's MONT, alongside actively attending national and Nordic training camps whenever possible.

2. Honorable Mention, Romanian Master of Mathematics 2025

Level of recognition: International

Type of award: Academic

Grade level when awarded: 11th grade

Award requirements: The strongest contestant from each Nordic country, based on a TST camp in December, is selected and together form team Nordics to participate in the Romanian Master of Mathematics. Then, at the contest, an HM is awarded to everyone with a full solve.

What you did to earn award: Getting selected to the RMM team means I scored the most out of Finnish candidates at the team selection test. At RMM, I produced a perfect solution to the first problem. I had not practiced for just the RMM; the problems here are different in character, and generally more difficult than the IMO which I prepared for almost daily since IMO 2024.

3. Gold Prize, 2025 Yau Mathcamp Best Project Award

Level of recognition: National; International

Type of award: Academic

Grade level when awarded: 11th grade

Award requirements: The gold prize is given to the top three Yau Mathcamp projects. Each project is by a team of three participants and involves research under a Mathcamp professor and presenting the results. Professors choose the recipients based on the presentation.

What you did to earn award: My project is in multidimensional diophantine approximation, which I was introduced to at the camp. My team worked on it full-time for two weeks at the camp, and produced a novel result. Then we paid attention to make the presentation not too involved, so that professors outside this specific field would still understand something.

7. Finalist, Datatahti National Programming Contest 2025

Level of recognition: National

Type of award: Academic

Grade level when awarded: 11th grade

Award requirements: This year, the top 23 contestants of the opening round advance to the finals. I received 18th place with 460 points in the opening round, and 14th place with 168 points in the finals. The finals were IOI-style.

What you did to earn award: I started doing CSES intermittently in August 2023, and I didn't train competitive programming outside of that.
